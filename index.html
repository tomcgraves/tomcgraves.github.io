About Modaly

The images we “see” when we look at clouds, and the patterns we discern in fire, water and other natural phenomena spring from random patterns of matter, transformed by our minds. 

Modaly attempts to do this with musical notes. Modality is a very old concept in music, dating back hundreds of years and still present in all forms of today’s music. Similar to scales, modes create “moods”, generally by including major or minor scale tones.  In Modaly, notes are chosen at random from modal arrays of notes, in groups of 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8,9,12 and 16 notes. The patterns create time signatures and the groups are repeated. When you hear a group of randomly-selected modal notes repeated, it creates a melody, so the randomness disappears and music occurs, created by your own musical sensibilities.
